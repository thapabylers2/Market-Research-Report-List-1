<p><strong>The Central Vascular Access Devices Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC)</li><li>Tunneled Catheters</li><li>Non Tunneled Catheters</li><li>Implanted Ports</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Central Vascular Access Devices market comprises several key types: </p><p>1. **Peripherally Inserted Central Catheters (PICC)** are inserted into a peripheral vein, providing long-term access for medication, fluids, and blood draws.</p><p>2. **Tunneled Catheters** are surgically placed beneath the skin for long-term access, often used for dialysis or chemotherapy.</p><p>3. **Non-Tunneled Catheters** are also inserted directly into a central vein for short-term use.</p><p>4. **Implanted Ports** are surgically placed devices beneath the skin, offering reliable access for repeated treatments while minimizing infection risk.</p></p>
