<p><p>The checked luggage scanner market is primarily divided into two categories: 2D and 3D scanners. 2D scanners generate two-dimensional images of luggage contents, allowing security personnel to visually inspect items. They are often faster and more cost-effective but may miss concealed threats. In contrast, 3D scanners create detailed three-dimensional images, providing a comprehensive view that enhances threat detection capabilities. While 3D scanners are generally more advanced, their higher cost and size can be limiting factors for some airports and security facilities.</p></p>
