<p><p>Coronary stent systems are medical devices used to maintain the patency of narrowed or blocked coronary arteries due to atherosclerosis. These systems typically consist of a mesh-like tube, known as a stent, which is inserted into the artery to keep it open after balloon angioplasty. <p><p>The coronary stent systems market is categorized into three main types: bare metal stents, drug-eluting stents, and fully bio-absorbable stents. Bare metal stents provide structural support to arteries, while drug-eluting stents release medication to prevent restenosis and improve long-term outcomes. Fully bio-absorbable stents are made from materials that dissolve over time, promoting natural vessel healing and reducing permanent implant concerns. Each type addresses specific clinical needs, influencing physician choice based on patient conditions and treatment goals.</p></p>
