<p><p>Creatine Kinase (CK) is an enzyme found in various tissues, and its market is categorized into three main isoforms: MM, MB, and BB. The MM type is primarily located in skeletal muscle and is most commonly associated with muscular disorders. The MB type is found mainly in the heart and is crucial for diagnosing myocardial infarctions. The BB type is present in the brain and smooth muscle, with implications in neurological disorders. These isoforms cater to different diagnostic and therapeutic needs in clinical settings.</p></p>
