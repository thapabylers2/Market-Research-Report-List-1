<p><p>The industrial microgrid market consists of two primary types: Grid-Tied Microgrids and Independent Microgrids. Grid-Tied Microgrids operate in conjunction with the larger utility grid, allowing for the exchange of energy and enhancing reliability during peak demand or outages. In contrast, Independent Microgrids function autonomously, providing energy security by relying on local generation sources. These systems cater to varying industrial needs, balancing efficiency, resiliency, and sustainability while supporting the transition to cleaner energy sources and enhancing operational flexibility.</p></p>
