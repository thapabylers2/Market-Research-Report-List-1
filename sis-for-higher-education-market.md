<p><p>The Student Information System (SIS) market in higher education is divided into two main types: on-premise SIS and cloud-based SIS. On-premise SIS involves software installed locally on a university's servers, offering greater control and customization but requiring significant IT resources for maintenance. In contrast, cloud-based SIS is hosted on remote servers and accessed via the internet, providing scalability, flexibility, and lower upfront costs. This allows institutions to focus on core activities while benefiting from regular updates and easier collaboration.</p></p>
